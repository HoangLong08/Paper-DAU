\section{The Optimization Gap}
\label{sec:r1}

Comparing population-based optimizers against a global optimum of the
thresholding criterion is not new. Hammouche \emph{et al.}~\cite{hammouche_eaai}
benchmarked six metaheuristics against an exhaustive optimum on gray-level images
and already observed that several fail to reach it as the threshold count grows,
and Merzban and Elbayoumi~\cite{merzban_eswa} showed an exact dynamic program
dominating metaheuristics for the Otsu criterion. We claim no novelty for the
comparison itself. Two elements are new here. First, uniform random search runs
at the \emph{same} asserted budget as every metaheuristic, which fixes the floor
that any purposeful optimizer must clear. Second, and central to this paper,
every solution is projected onto a clinically scored binary mask, so that a
residual gap in the objective can be tested for whether it reaches the patient at
all.

All nine search procedures consume an identical hard budget of $5{,}000$ function
evaluations, and all are referenced against the exact global optimum computed by
dynamic programming~\cite{menotti_ciarp}. We report the relative fitness gap, the
hit rate at the preregistered convergence tolerance $10^{-4}$, and the resulting
clinical Dice, at the primary operating point $\kprimary$ with the
\texttt{brightest} decoding rule.

Table~\ref{tab:main_grid} reports the outcome. The optimizers separate cleanly in
the objective's own units: at $\kprimary$ the hit rate ranges from $0.87$ (PSO)
down to $0.0005$ (random search), and across the full threshold grid \emph{no}
method reaches a hit rate of $0.99$ at any $k$, with the rate collapsing toward
zero as $k$ grows. This reproduces, on clinical data at a tolerance five orders
of magnitude tighter, the shortfall Hammouche \emph{et al.}~\cite{hammouche_eaai}
reported for $k>2$. The quantum-inspired variant is unremarkable within this
spread (hit rate $0.14$ at $\kprimary$) and never separates from the classical
metaheuristics.

The same table shows that this separation does not reach the mask. At the primary
operating point every method, including the exact optimum, yields a median Dice
within a narrow band, and the per-patient difference of each metaheuristic from
the exact solution is negligible. Formalizing this with paired equivalence tests
against the exact optimum, the median per-patient Dice difference is exactly zero
in all $36$ comparisons (two targets $\times$ two background settings $\times$
nine methods), mean differences lie within $[-0.0042, +0.0042]$ Dice, and
equivalence holds by two one-sided tests at the $\sesoilenient$ margin in $36$ of
$36$ comparisons and at the $\sesoistrict$ margin in $36$ of $36$; the smallest
margin at which equivalence can be declared is at most $0.007$ Dice across the
entire block. An optimizer can miss the objective badly enough that its hit rate
is zero, and its clinical mask remains statistically indistinguishable from the
mask of the exact global optimum. The distance the field competes to close does
not project onto the target.

\subsection{Decoupling Fitness from the Clinical Score}

% P2c (prereg §6/A4b): 5 lines of code, fully falsifiable, and it converts the
% "circularity" objection from a hole into a RESULT.
% Prediction, declared in advance: the correlation is approximately zero.
% If it is strong and positive, fitness IS a good proxy for Dice and the whole
% Goodhart argument weakens — that is what makes this a test rather than a demo.
We pre-specified a decoupling test: the rank correlation between an algorithm's
residual fitness gap and the magnitude of its Dice difference from the exact
solution, with the declared prediction that it would be approximately zero. That
prediction is \emph{not} supported. The per-cell Spearman correlation is
positive and moderate, ranging from $0.30$ to $0.77$ across configurations
(median near $0.60$) and declining as $k$ increases. Fitness therefore carries a
weak-to-moderate signal about the clinical score; it is not fully decoupled. We
report this against our prediction: the paper's thesis does not rest on complete
decoupling — Section~\ref{sec:r1} already shows the surviving gap is clinically
negligible even where the correlation is non-zero — but any statement that
fitness is uninformative about Dice would be false, and we do not make it.

\subsection{Mask Identity}

% The headline that needs no test: X% of (patient, k) cells where all algorithms
% emit BYTE-IDENTICAL masks. Report n_zero/n_total everywhere (A4d).
At the primary operating point the fraction of (patient, seed) cells on which
\emph{every} metaheuristic returns a byte-for-byte identical binary mask is
$0.001$ (Table~\ref{tab:main_grid}). This is a count, not an inference: it
requires no statistical test. Its small value is not a counterpoint to the
equivalence result but its complement — the masks are almost never literally
identical, yet their clinical scores are statistically equivalent, because the
handful of pixels on which the thresholds disagree fall where the decoding rule
and the tumor boundary do not.
